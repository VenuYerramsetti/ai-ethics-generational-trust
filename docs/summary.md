# Project Summary

## Overview
This project investigates how different generations perceive and respond to AI-generated content, including deepfakes, synthetic voices, and AI-generated text.

## Problem
Advances in generative AI have made it increasingly difficult to distinguish real from synthetic media, leading to risks such as misinformation, fraud, and erosion of trust.

## Key Insight
Different age groups experience AI differently:

- Older adults tend to trust visual and audio cues, making them vulnerable to manipulation.
- Younger users are more exposed but may become desensitized.
- Children face risks related to data privacy and behavioral profiling.

## Contribution
This work highlights:
- A generational perspective on AI trust
- Ethical risks of synthetic media
- The need for responsible AI governance

## Proposed Solutions
- AI watermarking and detection tools
- Policy frameworks for content labeling
- Public awareness and digital literacy programs

## Future Work
- User studies across demographics
- Development of detection tools
- Integration with policy research
